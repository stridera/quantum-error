# Mirror Shards

Five accessory pendants dropped by the echo fights in the [Spirit Dungeon](../../lore/dungeons/spirit-dungeon.md). Each shard is bound to the party member who defeated their own echo and cannot be equipped by anyone else.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Accessory (pendant) |
| **Quality** | Rare |
| **Slot** | Neck |
| **Soulbound** | Yes — bound on pickup to the echo's original |
| **Requires Attunement** | No (soulbinding handles it) |

> Mirror fragments set into simple frames. They do not shine; they reflect. Held up to a light source, they show what the bearer looked like the moment their echo died.

## Origin

Each shard is a fragment of the mirror wall in the Spirit Dungeon's [Changing Rooms](../../lore/dungeons/spirit-dungeon/rooms.md#room-2-changing-rooms-optional), broken off when an [echo](../../lore/dungeons/spirit-dungeon/mobs.md#mirror-shard-drops) was defeated. The system forges the fragment into a pendant as the echo dissipates. The shard is visible at the base of each mirror before the fight begins — the reward is known, but so is the cost.

Because the identity confrontation is what produces the shard, it cannot be looted, traded, or duplicated. A shard taken from another character's corpse becomes inert glass.

## Evolving Item

Mirror Shards are **evolving accessories**. The base effect scales with the bearer, gaining a small additional bonus at level milestones. The effects listed below are the drop-state values (Level 10 echoes, Rare quality).

**Milestones:** At Level 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, 60, 70, 80, 90, and 99, each shard gains one cumulative tier bump on its listed effects. Milestones trigger on first rest after reaching the level; the bearer briefly sees their own reflection move a half-second out of sync.

The shards do not rise in quality tier. They remain Rare forever — the growth is in the bearer, not the item. They are *identity* items as much as stat items; the milestone bumps keep them relevant without letting them eclipse gear earned later.

## The Shards

### Shard of Self — [Selene](../../characters/party/selene.md)
- **Base:** +1 Perception, +5% crit chance while in Veil or stealth (see [Veilstepper Rogue](../../classes/veilstepper-rogue.md))
- **Per milestone:** +1 Perception, +1% stealth crit
- *"It showed you who you were. Now it shows what's coming."*

### Shard of Devotion — [Wade](../../characters/party/wade.md)
- **Base:** +10% healing effectiveness on targets below 25% HP
- **Per milestone:** +2% low-HP healing effectiveness
- *"Your hands are steadier now."*

### Shard of Duty — [Clint](../../characters/party/clint.md)
- **Base:** +1 AR, +10% threat generation on taunt abilities
- **Per milestone:** +1 AR, +2% taunt threat
- *"The shield cracked. You didn't."*

### Shard of Control — [Vanessa](../../characters/party/vanessa.md)
- **Base:** −5% mana cost, +5% spell accuracy
- **Per milestone:** −1% mana cost, +1% spell accuracy
- *"You don't have to become anything else."*

### Shard of Purpose — [Rebekah](../../characters/party/rebekah.md)
- **Base:** +10% song and buff duration
- **Per milestone:** +2% song/buff duration
- *"Your voice carries further than you know."*

## Rules

- **Soulbinding** happens at pickup. Equipping by another character fails with *"This shard belongs to someone else."*
- **Persistence through death:** The shards behave like the [Fanny Pack of Holding](fanny-pack-of-holding.md) — they do not drop on death and return with the bearer on respawn.
- **Loss on repudiation:** If the bearer actively denies the identity truth their echo fight surfaced (GM call, not mechanical), the shard dims and the milestone bonuses pause until they face the lesson again. The base effect remains.
- **Inspection:** Holding a shard up and focusing shows the bearer a brief replay of the moment they landed the killing blow on their echo. This is a quiet mechanic, not an on-demand ability.

## Notes

- Wade's shard is only earned if he completes his echo fight. See the [echo encounter](../../lore/dungeons/spirit-dungeon/mobs.md) for failure behavior.
- Each shard is a narrative anchor — they should be referenced periodically in later chapters as a reminder of what the Spirit Dungeon cost.
- Milestone bumps are small on purpose. These are not BiS items; they are keepsakes that happen to have stats.
- After the echo fights, victors can return to their mirror for a private, narrative-only moment where the glass shows a glimpse of their future self. No mechanical effect — see [Story Beat: Looking Again](../../lore/dungeons/spirit-dungeon/rooms.md#story-beat-looking-again).
