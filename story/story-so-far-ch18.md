# The Story So Far

*A recap for readers picking up at Chapter 18.*

---

**Clint Henning** and four coworkers — Wade, Vanessa, Rebekah, and Dustin — discovered their world was running inside a *Universe System Simulator*, and that they could edit it. Using console access, Clint rebuilt them into the classes they wanted and pushed their stats and HP pools far past what any mortal should carry. Every primary attribute to 20, every HP bar to roughly 999. The one exception: Clint forgot his own **Luck**. It's still 3.

- **Wade** → **Cleric of Healing** (Level 6). His twin daughters were taken as leverage before the party could rescue them, and are still missing. He has sensed their direction ever since and runs toward them on willpower alone.
- **Rebekah** → **Temporal Bard** (Level 4). Her guitar was shot to pieces. She now plays on the *temporal strings themselves* — **Chrono Resonance** made visible, an invisible harp only she can pluck. Her songs don't deal damage, but she has one small exception: **Dust Note**, a plucked string that inflicts a second of accelerated decay on a single target.
- **Vanessa** → **Quantum Sorceress** (Level 5). Once panicked and set her own mana to 99,999, nearly dying of *Mana Lock*. Clint saved her but the damage etched permanent: **Mana Lucent**. Her veins and eyes now glow white, her spells cast at +2 levels, and she lives one bad cast away from burnout.
- **Dustin** → **Selene** (Level 5). A **Nekara** (cat-person) **Veilstepper Rogue**, the form she'd always wanted but never let herself have. She has not yet taken a real look at her own reflection.
- **Clint** → **Paladin of the System** (Level 3 — delayed because he ignored his notifications for days and missed the god selection prompt). A god named **Strider** eventually claimed him. Strider rides in his head, has opinions, and is not above puns.

The world has since flipped from Technology to Fantasy. Electricity died. Magic flooded in. Monsters spawned. The party died and respawned into a transformed San Francisco, most of their hand-crafted gear lost to Clint's first death (including his legendary katana and shield — he wasn't holding them when he burned).

Chasing Wade south toward where he could feel his kids, the group stumbled into a glowing Halloween store standing impossibly pristine in the crumbling zone. Once inside, they couldn't leave.

**[Spirit Dungeon](../lore/dungeons/spirit-dungeon.md), Level 10.** A pop-up nightmare wearing the skin of a seasonal retail chain, every room another horror trope made literal. The party is underleveled (L3–L6 vs L10 enemies), but their absurd HP pools are carrying them through. They've fought through Jason and Freddy copies, Pinhead and Scream casters, a bear trap that dropped an actual bear on Clint's chest, and a trio of sexy-costume mannequins (pirate, nurse, witch) that nearly killed Vanessa when she rushed in to disarm the trap herself. She hasn't forgiven herself yet. Clint reminded her of a D&D lesson they'd both forgotten: *trust your group. They're there to save you — even from yourself.*

Clint's clothes burned off in the first fight. He's wearing a towel, a *Singed Fedora*, and a *Robe of the Silent Scream* pulled from a mannequin. His weapon is a plastic bloody machete that somehow still draws blood. Wade is wearing a tiny nurse's cap for the +5% healing bonus. None of them have quite stopped laughing about it.

---

The party has just stepped out of the employee hallway. Ahead of them, past a short costume aisle, is a small hexagonal room made entirely of mirrors.

None of them have had a clean look at themselves since the Transition.

Selene least of all.
